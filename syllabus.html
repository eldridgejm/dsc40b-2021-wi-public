  Homeworks

There will be eight homeworks assigned throughout the quarter. Homeworks will be a mixture of written problems (which are manually graded by our tutor staff) and coding problems (which are autograded). Each homework will be due via Gradescope at 11:59 PM on the Tuesday after it is assigned, and you'll have roughly a week to complete each assignment from the time it is posted. The lowest homework will be dropped.

Homework Redemption

DSC 40B utilizes the concept of "redemption" on homework assignments. Under this policy, you may regain full credit for a homework problem that was previously done incorrectly by submitting an explanation of your mistake along with a correction. This policy encourages you to revisit homework mistakes in order to correct your understanding, and allows us to give quick, targeted feedback through grading.

Not all homework problems are eligible for redemption, unfortunately, as we do not have the resources to regrade longer, more involved problems. Those problems that are eligible for redemption will be marked as such. The policy of dropping the lowest homework serves to offer some relief in the case that you perform poorly on problems that are not eligible for redemption.

Problems that are eligible for redemption are graded as either right or wrong with no partial credit according to the following scale:

  • Green: You had the right idea, and you get full credit for the problem.
  • Yellow: Your approach showed effort, but wasn't right. For now it will receive zero credit, but you can receive full credit by submitting a proper "redemption request" (see below).
  • Red: You didn't answer the problem, or showed no work. No credit is awarded and you are not permitted to submit a redemption request.

If you got the problem correct, you'll receive the total number of points for it. If you didn't get the problem correct, even due to a relatively small mistake, you'll receive no credit until you submit a redemption request. In this way, everyone will eventually receive full credit on every problem that is eligible for redemption, provided that they submit redemption requests.

Redemption Requests

To submit a redemption request for a Yellow solution:

  1. Review your answer to find your mistake, comparing it to the solutions posted on the course webpage.
  2. Submit a regrade request for the problem on Gradescope, or by attending a tutor's office hours. In your regrade request, identify your mistake and what you would need to do to correct it. The amount of detail needed in your request depends on how complex your mistake was; if it was a simple one, only one or two sentences may be necessary.
  3. A grader will review your request shortly (as long as you submit it within a week of the homework scores being posted, your regrade request will be reviewed). If you aren't able to identify what you did incorrectly, you'll be asked to attend a grader's office hours in order to discuss the problem in more detail.
Warning! We will not be able to handle redemption requests which are submitted more than a week after you have received your grade.

Slip Days

You have four "slip days" to use throughout the quarter. A slip day extends the deadline of any one homework by 24 hours. Slip days can't be "stacked" — the latest any one homework can be submitted is 24 hours after the deadline. Slip days are applied automatically at the end of the quarter, but it's your responsibility to keep track of how many you have left.

Slip days are designed to be a transparent and predictable source of leniency in deadlines. You can use a slip day if you are too busy to complete an assignment on its original due date (or if you forgot about it). But slips days are also meant for things like the internet going down at 11:58 PM just as you go to submit your homework. Slip days are to be used in exceptional circumstances, so you probably shouldn't get close to using all four — if you feel that you will need that many due to a reason like illness or depression, send me a message and we'll figure something out.

Collaboration

Starting with Homework 03, you may work on homeworks in pairs and submit one assignment for the both of you. Note that you should both work on each problem together; you shouldn't divvy up the problems, so that one of you solves problems 1-3 and the other solves 4-6. Of course, we have no idea of knowing how you worked on the homeworks, so a word of warning: the homeworks are meant to be preparation for the exams, so if you only understand half of the homework problems, you should expect a score ~50% on the exams.

Here's the recommended way for working on the assignments as a pair. You should each take a look at the homework problems separately and attempt your own solution. Then you can meet over Zoom to discuss each problem and write up your solution document together.

Working in a pair is optional. If you'd like to find someone to work with, we'll be making a Campuswire post where you can request a partner.

Regrade Requests

If you feel that the grader has made a mistake, you may submit a regrade request via Gradescope within one week of the grades being released. Note that part of your grade is clarity, so if your answer was mostly right but unclear you may still not deserve full credit.

Any problem, whether or not it is eligible for redemption, is eligible for a regrade request if you feel that the grader has made a mistake.

  Exams

There will be two midterms and one final exam, held on the following dates:

  • Midterm 01: Tuesday, November 10 (covers Lectures 1 — 7)
  • Midterm 02: Thursday, December 03 (covers Lectures 8 — 15)
  • Final Exam: Wednesday, December 16

The exams will not be at a specific time. Instead, there will be a window of 24 hours during which you can start your exam, but once your exam is started you will have a limited amount of time to finish it: 1.5 hours for the midterm and 3 hours for the final exam.

Exam Redemption

DSC 40B utilizes the concept of exam "redemption". Under this policy, a bad performance on an earlier exam can be erased by good performance on the same material in a later exam.

Redemption is implemented as follows. The final exam will be split into three parts:

  1. The mandatory final exam section, covering Lectures 16 — 18
  2. An optional Midterm 01 "Redemption" section covering Lectures 01 — 07
  3. An optional Midterm 02 "Redemption" section covering Lectures 08 — 15

If your score on the midterm redemption section is higher than your score on the original midterm, it will replace that grade. Getting a lower score on a redemption section cannot hurt you (but it will make me sad). As a consequence, the redemption sections are optional.

Example: You got an "F" on Midterm 1 and a "B" on Midterm 2. You decide to take only the first redemption section on the final (though you could have taken both), and you receive an "A". Your midterm scores are now "A" and "B".

Passing Threshold

In order to pass the class, you must get at least 60% on both midterm exams (or the equivalent redemption sections). That is, if you do not pass a midterm in either your first attempt or in the redemption attempt, you cannot pass the class. Note that this is the case even if your other grades are good. For example, if you receive an "A" on the first midterm, have a homework average of "A", but receive an "F" on both the second midterm and its redemption section, you cannot pass the class.

The reason behind this policy is that receiving a non-passing grade on both the midterm and its redemption section indicates a significant misunderstanding in a core aspect of the course; since DSC 40B is a core course in the data science major, passing with such a gap in understanding will likely affect your ability to understand and succeed in future courses.

  Grading Scheme

We'll be using the following grading scheme:

  • 35%: Homework Assignments (lowest dropped)
  • 25%: Midterm 01 (or Redemption Midterm 01, whichever is larger)
  • 25%: Midterm 02 (or Redemption Midterm 02, whichever is larger)
  • 15%: Final Exam Mandatory Section

The final exam mandatory section is worth a relatively small percentage of your grade because it covers only 4 lectures.

You must score at least 60% on each of the midterms in order to pass the course (see above).

In a typical quarter, the midterm redemption policy has the same effect as a traditional "curve", therefore replacing the need for one. Once all scores are in, I will run a clustering algorithm to automatically find the best cutoffs for each letter grade, but these cutoffs can only be lowered. For instance, the threshold for an "A" will never be higher than 93%.

  Illnesses and Extenuating Circumstances

Because of the pandemic, we must prepare for the unfortunate possibility that you will get sick and be unable to participate in this class for long periods of time. The university has a mechanism for helping in this situation: the Incomplete. If you are unable to complete the course because of reasons outside of your control, you may be given an Incomplete instead of a letter grade. This simply means that you will complete the rest of the work at a later time. Once you have done so, your overall grade is calculated and your Incomplete grade is replaced.

An Incomplete does not allow you to re-do work that has already been completed, only to do work that hasn't been completed.

Unfortunately, your personal health is not the only thing that might prevent you from participating in this class. Some of us will get sick, others will have family members fall ill, and others might lose their jobs. If you have any doubt about your ability to perform satisfactorily in this course due to something outside of your control, please contact me ASAP and we'll figure something out.

  Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a webcam for this class?

Nope! If you have one, you're welcome to use it; it can be especially useful to share your work during office hours. But we won't require webcams. In particular, we are not using proctoring services like examity.

What happens if the professor gets sick and can't run the course?

We've prepared for this possibility in a couple of ways. Namely:

  1. "Backup" faculty have been selected who are ready to take over the course.
  2. All grades will be stored in Canvas so that they are accessible to the university.

Is this class curved?

In a typical quarter, the midterm redemption policy has the same effect as a traditional "curve", therefore replacing the need for one. Once all scores are in, I will run a clustering algorithm to automatically find the best cutoffs for each letter grade, but these cutoffs can only be lowered. For instance, the threshold for an "A" might be lowered to a 92.5%, but it will never be raised above 93%.
